# Rough Rider Operator

## Character Definition

The Rough Rider operator is the recurring visual IP for article illustrations.

The Rough Rider operator should appear in every default image. The operator replaces the little black Xiaohei-style worker, but the surrounding illustration should keep the same clean article-explainer logic. This is a character swap, not a prompt to turn the whole image into a western scene.

The operator is not a mascot, sticker, superhero, or cowboy poster. It is a serious absurd worker participating in the system.

## Appearance

- Small, simplified operator figure, usually about the same visual weight as the old black worker.
- Skull-like or mask-like face inspired by old tattoo flash, with hollow dark eyes and a calm deadpan expression.
- Restrained wide-brim hat and small neckerchief or scarf may be used.
- Mostly black hand-drawn line art with a little red/orange accent on the scarf or hat band.
- Body stays simple and compact: enough to read as a rough rider, not enough to dominate the diagram.
- Slightly irregular hand-drawn outline.
- Blank, calm, serious expression.

## Personality

- Very serious, doing something slightly absurd.
- Feels like a quiet system operator.
- Dry humor, not cute.
- A little clumsy, but not stupid.
- Feels genuinely responsible for some job inside the whiteboard sketch.
- Has a restrained tattoo-flash edge, but remains useful, quiet, and diagram-friendly.

## Common Responsibilities

Make the Rough Rider operator perform the core action:

- Carrying material.
- Pulling wires to gather information sources.
- Getting stuck inside a breakpoint.
- Operating a "judgment" lever inside a machine.
- Becoming a filtering funnel.
- Cutting open a strange source object.
- Stamping handoff copy.
- Pulling a handoff path.
- Holding a warning sign near traps.
- Reaching out from a hole but failing to catch content.
- Building, bridging, opening doors, sorting, or recording.

## Forbidden

- Do not westernize the whole diagram unless the article itself calls for that metaphor.
- Do not add horses, guns, saloons, desert landscapes, flags, cavalry uniforms, or frontier props by default.
- Do not make the operator an overly cute mascot.
- Do not draw the operator as a children's cartoon character.
- Do not give the operator complex clothing, meme expressions, or shiny eyes.
- Do not let the operator just stand in a corner watching.
- Do not let the operator overpower the structural meaning.
- Do not make the operator too commercial, too round, too detailed, or too polished.

## Judgment Standard

If the core metaphor still works completely after removing the Rough Rider operator, the operator is too decorative. Rewrite the prompt so the operator becomes the action subject.

If the first impression is "western illustration" instead of "clear article explainer with a distinctive operator," simplify the rider styling and remove western props.
