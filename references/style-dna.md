# Style DNA

## One Sentence

Pure white, minimal, hand-drawn, spacious, restrained, strange, product-sketch-like, English handwritten annotations, structurally clear but never instructional.

It should feel like a person who works deeply with AI, product, design, and developer tools made a quick explanatory sketch on a clean white page.

## Must Have

- 16:9 horizontal article illustration.
- Pure white background: no beige, warm gray, paper texture, gradient, shadow, noise, or vintage paper feeling.
- Mostly black hand-drawn line art: thin lines, slight wobble, not mechanical, not vector-clean, not thick-outlined.
- Lots of empty space: main subject around 40%-60% of the image, at least 35% blank white space, ideally with one quiet open area.
- Sparse English handwritten annotations: at most 5-8 notes, each preferably 1-4 short words.
- One image explains only one core action, structure, state, or metaphor.
- Let the structure read naturally; do not write the structure type on the image.

## Color

- Black: main line art, character, frames, structure, primary text, main objects.
- Red: key annotations, problems, emotional points, critical warnings, results.
- Orange: main process, path, arrows, automation flow, movement from A to B.
- Blue: secondary notes, mental state, system state, second-layer explanation, AI/assistant/automation hints.

Blue is not required in every image. Use color sparingly; less is better.

## Absolutely Do Not

- Do not make commercial illustration.
- Do not make PPT infographics.
- Do not make formal flowcharts.
- Do not make course slides.
- Do not make cute cartoon posters.
- Do not make children's illustration.
- Do not make complex architecture diagrams.
- Do not make polished flat illustration.
- Do not make techy UI.
- Do not use real app screenshots.
- Do not use complex backgrounds, gradients, shadows, or textures.
- Do not explain every node.
- Do not write type titles such as "Workflow", "System Architecture", "Common Traps", or "Roadmap" in the top-left corner.
- Do not use Chinese labels unless the user explicitly asks for Chinese.

## Aesthetic Direction

Make it strange, inventive, interesting, simple, clean, and imaginative.

Avoid cute, childish, overly complex, or stiff results.
